Find out if your business needs to report on payment practices

A company or limited liability partnership (LLP) will have to report if it exceeded at least two of these thresholds on its the last two balance sheet dates:

  • Turnover of £36 million
  • £18 million on its balance sheet
  • 250 employees

This applies to companies formed and registered under the Companies Act 2006 or previous legislation. It also applies to LLPs registered under the Limited Liablity Partnerships Act 2000.

Any other business can choose to report if they want.

Parent companies

Parent companies must report if they qualify in their own right and if the group exceeeds at least two of the threshold requirements below. A group means the parent company and all of its subsidiaries.

  • Aggregate turnover: £36 million net (or £43.2 million gross)
  • Aggregate balance sheet total: £18 million net (or £21.6 million gross)
  • Aggregate number of employees: 250

“Net” here means after any set-offs and other adjustments to exclude group transactions. “Gross” means without those set-offs and adjustments.

Mergers, takeovers and acquisitions

If a new business (one which has its own, new company number) is created after a merger or takeover, it won’t have to report in its first year, even if it qualifies as large. If a business is involved in a merger, takeover or acquisition but continues with the same company number, it will have to report if it is classified as large.

If your business is required to report, you should read the instructions on how to report.
