Which invoices you need to report on

You need to report on all invoices apart from those involving contracts for financial services, and those where the contracts don’t have a significant connection to the UK.

What you need to report

Start and end date of reporting period

  • These will be the dates given above under ‘Your Deadlines’.

Average time to pay in days

  • Out all of the payments you have made in the reporting period, the average time it took to pay. This must be rounded up or down to the nearest whole number.

Percentage of invoices not paid within agreed terms

  • This relates to the number of invoices, not their value. This must be rounded up or down to the nearest whole number.

Percentage of invoices paid

  • Within 30 days
  • In 31 to 60 days
  • In more than 60 days

Round these to the nearest whole number.

Standard Payment Terms

  • This should include your standard payment period, an explain any special terms and requirements. If you have different terms for different types of supplier, explain them here. Most companies write a couple of paragraphs here. You can include a link to the relevant page or PDF on your website.

Your maximum contractual payment period

  • The longest payment terms you demanded from a supplier in this reporting period. You can simply enter the number of days, or can add an explanation as well.

Changes to standard terms

  • This should include details of exactly what has changed – be sure to include the ‘before’ and ‘after’ situation. You’ll also need to tell us whether you consulted with suppliers before making changes, and informed them of the changes before they took effect.
